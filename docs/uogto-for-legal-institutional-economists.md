# UOGTO for Legal and Institutional Economists

Use UOGTO when the analytic problem is shaped by rules, institutions, incentives, allocations, and contractual or governance constraints.

## What UOGTO captures well

- Auctions, bargaining, matching, and mechanism design
- Contractual and institutional rule structures
- Norms, compliance, and governance-driven interaction
- Allocation rules and incentive constraints
- Traceable examples that support article claims about institutional behavior

## Best-fit modules

- `ontologies/extensions/mechanism-design.ttl`
- `ontologies/extensions/auctions.ttl`
- `ontologies/extensions/bargaining.ttl`
- `ontologies/extensions/matching-allocation.ttl`
- `ontologies/extensions/contract-theory.ttl`
- `ontologies/extensions/institutional-economics.ttl`
- `ontologies/extensions/deontic-logic.ttl`
- `ontologies/extensions/computational-social-choice.ttl`

## Recommended reuse pattern

Start from the mechanism or institution, then add the strategic interaction and observed outcome. Keep the model explicit about rule constraints, allocation logic, and what is being treated as an institution rather than a pure strategic game.
