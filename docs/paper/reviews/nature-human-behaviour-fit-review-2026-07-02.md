# Nature Human Behaviour Fit Review

Date: 2026-07-02

Reviewer agent: `019f2129-d105-7372-809a-1693a1c6c056` (`Jason`)

## Verdict

UOGTO is not a strong fit as a standard Nature Human Behaviour Article because it does not report a primary behavioural finding. It is more plausibly framed as a Resource: a reusable ontology and evidence package for behavioural, social, economic, computational, AI-agent, and policy researchers who model strategic interaction.

## Fix-Now Findings

- Replace the mistaken Nature Genetics / genomic-medicine framing with Nature Human Behaviour logic.
- Treat genomic medicine policy as a secondary downstream example, not a venue-defining application.
- Reframe the manuscript as a resource for strategic-interaction evidence in behavioural and computational research.
- Make the first three paragraphs accessible to behavioural scientists before introducing RDF/OWL/SHACL detail.
- Add a behavioural/policy utility subsection explaining how UOGTO helps answer who acted, what choices were available, what incentives operated, what outcome was observed, and what evidence supports the claim.
- Expand Data and Code Availability with repository URL, generated artefacts, commands, and access restrictions.
- Add declarations covering ethics, competing interests, funding, author contributions, acknowledgements, and AI/tool use.

## Deferred Items

- Prepare Nature Human Behaviour reporting-summary materials if targeting that venue.
- Add a more detailed behavioural case study such as public goods, collective risk, social norms, bargaining, or AI-mediated interaction.
- Finalize article type, author metadata, funding, competing interests, repository DOI/version, and cover-letter framing.

## Rejected Framing

- Do not pitch UOGTO as a genetics, genomic medicine, clinical utility, health-economics, or empirical human-behaviour finding.
- Do not imply that UOGTO validates behavioural mechanisms. It supports inspectability and reuse of strategic-interaction assumptions.

## Applied Outcome

The manuscript was updated to:

- retitle the work as a reusable strategic-interaction evidence resource;
- foreground behavioural/social/computational/policy relevance;
- add a behavioural and policy utility subsection;
- remove genetics-first positioning;
- clarify that UOGTO is not a theory of human behaviour;
- strengthen Data and Code Availability and add draft Declarations.
