## Official Requirement Themes Checked

Nature Human Behaviour:

- Nature Human Behaviour covers individual and collective human behaviour across psychology, economics, neuroscience, sociology, anthropology, political science, public policy, AI, decision-making, and related fields.
- The best fit for UOGTO is a Resource-style contribution: a reusable tool or dataset-like artefact of broad utility, interest, and significance, rather than an Article reporting a primary behavioural finding.
- Nature Portfolio journals require transparent data availability; code availability is expected where custom code is needed to replicate or build on conclusions.
- Nature Portfolio submissions require competing-interest disclosure, ethics clarity where applicable, author contribution/declaration materials, and accessible framing for adjacent-field readers.

Medical Decision Making:

- Authors should write for a sophisticated general medical readership using clear, concise, logically organized scientific writing.
- Manuscript types include original research, reviews, tutorials, technical notes, explainers, and case studies.
- MDM emphasizes integration, synthesis, interpretation, methodologic relevance, clinical/policy decision applications, and ICMJE-aligned authorship/reporting expectations.

Safety-oriented outlets:

- Safety-oriented author guidance emphasizes clear article structure, methods, results, discussion, conclusions, ethics, conflicts of interest, author contributions, open access, reproducibility, figure/table clarity, and complete datasets where possible.
- Safety venues prioritize relevance to safety, resilience, systems, risk, and practice domains.
